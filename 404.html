Moon to Earth

The Moon is drifting away The Moon moves about 3.8 cm (1.5 inches) away from Earth every year. Scientists think this will eventually slow Earth's rotation.

Moonquakes are real Just like Earth has earthquakes, the Moon has "moonquakes." These are caused by tidal forces and can last up to an hour.

The Moon smells like gunpowder Apollo astronauts said lunar dust smelled like gunpowder when they returned to their spacecraft.

There's water on the Moon Scientists have discovered ice in craters at the Moon's poles, meaning there's frozen water in places that never see sunlight.
The Moon once had active volcanoes Though it's mostly inactive now, evidence shows there were volcanic eruptions on the Moon as recently as 100 million years ago.
The Moon has its own time zones Scientists have proposed a "Lunar Standard Time" because time runs slightly faster on the Moon due to its weaker gravity.

Return to Earth: Simple Explanation

Leaving the Moon

After spending about 21 hours on the moon, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin launched back into space using the top part of the lunar module. They joined Michael Collins, who was waiting for them in the command module orbiting the moon.

Journey Back

The crew began a 3-day trip back to Earth, traveling at high speed. They adjusted their spacecraft to enter Earth’s atmosphere at the right angle.

Re-entry and Landing

Suit leak checks. Seat harnessing and fit checks. Communication tests with mission control.

Rescue and Quarantine

A U.S. Navy ship picked them up. They were placed in quarantine for 21 days to make sure they didn’t bring back any unknown germs from the moon.

Celebration

After quarantine, they were welcomed as heroes and celebrated worldwide for their incredible achievement.
