The Saturn V had a total of 11 main engines, distributed across its three stages. Each played a crucial role in getting the spacecraft to the Moon. Here's a summary of each:
1st Stage (S-IC) – F-1 Engines
Number: 5 F-1 engines
Total thrust: 34.5 million Newtons (~3,500 tons of thrust)
Fuel: RP-1 (refined kerosene) + liquid oxygen
Duration: ~2.5 minutes

  • 2nd Stage (S-II) – J-2 Engines
  • Number: 5 J-2 engines
  • Total thrust: 5 million Newtons (~500 tons of thrust)
  • Fuel: Liquid hydrogen + liquid oxygen
  • Duration: ~6 minutes
  • Function: Continue accelerating the rocket after separation of the first stage.

  • 3rd Stage (S-IVB) – J-2 Engine
  • Quantity: 1 J-2 engine
  • Thrust: 1 million Newtons (~100 tons of thrust)
  • Fuel: Liquid hydrogen + liquid oxygen
